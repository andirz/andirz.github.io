## What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website.  
They contain information that allows websites to recognize devices, store preferences, or enable certain functions.

Cookies can be:
- **Session cookies**, which are deleted automatically when the browser is closed
- **Persistent cookies**, which remain stored for a defined period of time

Cookies may be set by the website itself (**first-party cookies**) or by external services (**third-party cookies**).

---

## Why are cookies used?

Cookies are commonly used for purposes such as:
- Ensuring website functionality and security
- Remembering user preferences
- Analyzing website traffic and usage
- Measuring the effectiveness of content
- Displaying relevant advertisements

Some cookies are technically required, while others are optional and require user consent.

---

## Types of cookies

### 1. Technically necessary cookies

These cookies are required for the basic operation of a website.  
They ensure that pages load correctly, security mechanisms function, and content can be delivered.

---

### 2. Analytics cookies

Analytics cookies collect information about how visitors use a website.  
This data helps website operators understand user behavior and improve content and performance.

---

### 3. Marketing and advertising cookies

Marketing cookies are used to track visitors across websites and display advertisements based on user interests.

These cookies are optional and require explicit user consent.

When cookies are collected, small pieces of information are stored in the browser to recognize interactions, preferences, or usage patterns.

Depending on the cookie type, this may include:
- Technical session identifiers
- Website usage statistics
- Advertising-related interactions

On this website, cookies for analytics or advertising are used **only after explicit consent**.
